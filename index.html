ABOUT ME

Hi! I’m Charlie Yin, a software developer currently based in Vancouver, British Columbia.

I graduated from the University of British Columbia, receiving a Bachelor of Electrical Engineering in 2017. Even though I studied as an electrical engineer, I actually became more and more interested in the software side of things. I’ve worked on different projects throughout the years, some of which include developing firmware for robots, implementing distributed real-time networks, and even making an AI for learning text patterns. I'm most proficient in C/C++, though I do have a good working knowledge of JavaScript, Python, and many more. My current interest lies in firmware development and machine learning/artificial intelligence.

Aside from software, I also have a big interest in music. I listen to a ton of jazz, with recordings dating back all the way to the 40's, and I'll frequently practice jazz piano as a side hobby.

PROJECTS
I’ve had a chance to work on some truly interesting projects over the years. This includes things such as autonomous sailboats, self-balancing robots, personal webpage, etc. This section highlights some of the most memorable and important ones that I’ve worked on. Non-mobile users can hover over each image for a short clip of the project.
  • UBC Sailbot

    UBC’s autonomous sailboat. I worked with a team to implement a distributed real-time network in C/C++ for sensor data transfer over the CAN bus using the CANopen protocol. The result is a reliable network which can support up to 127 separate nodes.

  • Gyro Stabilized Platform

    A self-stabilizing platform. I implemented the software in C++ which allowed the platform to self-stabilize within +/-1° of the horizontal in less than 2s with an overshoot or undershoot of no more than +/-4°, all while supporting a weight of up to 200g.

  • Self-Correcting Helicopter

    A self-correcting helicopter model. I worked with a partner to implement the PID control in C++ necessary for the 2-DOF helicopter to recover from external disturbances in both the pitch and yaw directions. The final product recovers from any external disturbance quickly and with minimal oscillations.

  • Personal Homepage

    A modern and interactive homepage written by me. The page features a responsive design and automatically scales and adapts to any reasonable device screen size while maintaining full functionality. It provides a smooth browsing experience and is supported by most major browsers in use today.
